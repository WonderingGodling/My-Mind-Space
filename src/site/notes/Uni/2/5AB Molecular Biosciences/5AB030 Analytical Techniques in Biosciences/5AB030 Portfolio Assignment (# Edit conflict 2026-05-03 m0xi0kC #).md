#### 1.0 INTRODUCTION
- Talk what cell culture is and why is it important in biomedical research
A cell culture is a growing group of cells in a controlled environment—usually foreign to the cells (Weiskirchen et al., 2023). Cell cultures allow for the monitoring of the cells under certain conditions such as identifying the viability of cells when exposed to differing levels of toxicity. 
- Introduce your experiment – determine the toxicity of an unknown drug using its effects on cell viability
In this experiment varying concentrations of drug "alyteserin-1b" were created to be exposed to pancreatic cells to test its efficacy on decreasing the cells viability
- Why is it important to determine toxicity
It is important to determine the toxicity of a substance so that the people who would by distributing or promoting it know how safe the substance is. If it is safe at all, the nature and severity of any toxic effects will help determine the dosage and the treatment method for when a person shows symptoms of those effects. (Fischer, Milton and Wallace, 2020)
- Why is cell viability a good measure of toxicity?
Cell viability is used as a measure for toxicity because it has a lot of guidelines surrounding how to carry out and interpret tests for it; it gets used by a lot of microbiologists and has a fairly trust-worthy reputation as accurate (Khalef et al., 2024;Riss et al., 2016). The idea is that if a cell's metabolic activity is insufficient following exposure to a substance it can be assumed that it was (if statistically significant) because of the substance. If a cell can be made to inviable by a drug then this method deems it to be toxic.
- MTT assay will be used. Why is MTT assay a good assay technique (talk briefly about the mechanism of MTT assay)
In the MTT assay, the MTT gets metabolised by cells, indicating mitochondrial activity(van Meerloo, Kaspers and Cloos, 2011;Riss et al., 2016).  If the metabolism of the solution used alongside the MTT leads to a decrease or termination of metabolism then it can be concluded that there was a cytotoxic effect within the cell.
#### 2.0 MATERIALS AND METHODS

2.2 Methods
2.2.1 Seeding of cells (summary of Week 1)
100μl of cell suspension and 100μl of trypan blue were inserted into a haemocytometer to get a concentration of viable cells per ml. That concentration was then used to calculate the volume needed for 50,000 cells. The 12.5ml value that was derived was then mixed with 37.5 DMEM–the growth medium which allows for an accurate result across the 24 wells used in this experiment–creating the solution which would be pipetted into wells. The two well plates were then placed into a CO₂ incubator (at 37°C) for a week for the seeded cells to attach properly.
2.2.2 Treatment of cells (summary of week 2)
After a week (and after the well plates had been emptied of waste, washed with PBS, and aspirated), 4 serial dilutions (1/6) of alyteserin-1b were transferred into each of the wells. There was a 1 in 10 dilution of DMSO for the positive control. Nothing but media was in the negative control. These plates went back into the incubator (at 37°C) for a week.
2.2.3 MTT Assay (Summary of week 3)
The plates were removed from the incubator before each well (of last weeks plates and a new "blank" plate) had 1ml of MTT introduced into it . All plates were gently swirled so that the solution inside each was homogenous when it returned to the incubator (at 37°C) for 2 hours. The media was then removed via Pasteur pipette without disturbing the cells. Then, 0.5ml of MTT solvent was incorporated into each well, the plates were wrapped in tin foil and then they were placed on top of an orbital shaker for 15 minutes. Following the 15 minutes, 150μl was transferred from each well to a well of a higher capacity (96 well) plate to be read together at an absorbance of 590nm.

2.3 Statistical analysis
Data were analysed using GraphPad Prism . Values are Mean ± SEM with n = 4. Datasets were compared using One Way Analysis of Variance with Dunnett’s post hoc test. Data were considered to be statistically significantly different if P<0.05.
