A young (early 20’s) and airheaded human paladin raised most of her life as a maid for the Fontaine house, only recently picking up the capability for fighting. Raised on folklore and legends, she has an almost unrealistic adventurous spirit, seeing the opportunity to guard Everett as a way to live out her fantasy of following the footsteps of her heroes.

Extremely chivalrous and knightly approach to everyone, as well as an extremely caring and helpful person, being a lot more casual for those who get close to her, dropping the exalted persona subconsciously. A knight errant passionate about their journey, just as prone to any potential conflict and realizations that the real world is not as simple as she may think.

- People Who Are Very High Class Can Recognise Sinclairs "Servential Body Language"
	- Sinclaire Knows Folk Lore
		- Write More For Her To Know
	- Sinclaire Is Treated With Bare Minimum Respect In The Settlement

- Daughter of a maid working for Everett’s family, always shown respect but the power difference in the house was very clear
    
- Grew up spending a lot of her time with her mother, who often told Sinclair various pieces of folklore and other stories
    

- Through said stories, Sinclair picked up on the behaviors and perspectives shared in them, which heavily impacted the view on the world she held
    

- Within other children (such as the ones of other maids and noblefolks), was an outcast, since she didn’t exactly share their careless and simple behavior (as one would expect of a child), making her seem somewhat distanced
    
- Would go on to befriend Everett at a young age, with their friendship growing over the years
    
- With Everett choosing Sinclair as their bodyguard, she needed to quickly learn how to actually perform the duty of a guard. For this reason, a friend of Everett’s family who is a retired knight was called upon to train her
    
- Within the training Sinclair found a new mentor figure, though through it she also saw her first glimpse into the reality that her fantasies were in fact simply fantasies, as her new mentor would often highlight that she should hope to never use what she learnt here
    
- Once a couple of months passed, Sinclair’s mentor decided she’s ready to serve her duty well enough, and with that she and Everett left off
